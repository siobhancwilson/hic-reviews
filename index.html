How facilities are linked across data sources

Each data source uses its own facility naming convention. The HIC platform matches every facility to the national Master Health Facility List (MHFL) and uses the MHFL name as the canonical display name across all dashboards.

  • A facility called “Siphosesimbi CHC” in TIER.Net and D2D may appear as “Siphosesimbi Clinic” (the MHFL name) in reports
  • If you cannot find a facility by the name you know from a specific source system, try searching for variations or use the Facility Reconciliation page to look up the mapping
  • DHIS ward-level data (service delivery points below facility level) is aggregated to the parent facility
  • Facilities not yet registered in the MHFL appear as “not in MFL” with their source system name
  • The MHFL is updated daily but reflects the national registry — there may be a lag before newly opened or reclassified facilities appear

Facility matching is reviewed and updated monthly. Contact the HIC data team if you notice a facility under the wrong district or missing entirely.

Considerations
  • Stock variables (e.g. total HIV infections, population, ART coverage) represent values at mid-year.
  • Flow variables (e.g. new HIV infections, AIDS deaths, births, infant mortality) cover the period from mid-year to mid-year; for example, AIDS deaths in 1995 refer to mid-1995–mid-1996.

  • 95% confidence intervals (CIs) capture uncertainty in HIV-related parameters (transmission, progression, mortality, testing) but exclude demographic uncertainty (fertility, migration, non-HIV mortality) and possible future intervention changes (e.g. PrEP uptake).
  • The national model is calibrated separately from provincial models because many input data are available only at national or nationally age-disaggregated levels.

Reference & Supplementary

Facility master lists, population estimates, targets, community monitoring, and private sector data that supplement the core programme sources.

Master Health Facility List (MHFL)

NDoH
South Africa's canonical public health facility registry. Provides facility names, types, GPS coordinates, and organizational hierarchy (province/district/sub-district). Every data source in the HIC platform is matched to the MHFL to enable cross-source analysis at a common geographic level. Updated daily as facilities open, close, or change classification.
GranularityFacility
RefreshDaily
Considerations
  • The MHFL reflects the national registry and may lag behind operational reality — newly opened, closed, or reclassified facilities can take weeks to appear or update.
  • Facility names in the MHFL may differ from what source systems use (e.g. “Clinic” vs “CHC”, abbreviated vs full names). The MHFL name is used as the canonical display name across all HIC dashboards.
  • Some facilities in source systems (particularly small NGO clinics, mobile units, and condom distribution sites) may never appear in the MHFL. These are flagged as “not in MFL” in reports.
  • GPS coordinates in the MHFL may be approximate — some were geocoded from addresses rather than captured on-site.

HRIS

NDoH (HISP)
Human Resources Information System integrated with HRID (PEPFAR Human Resources Inventory Database). Tracks health workforce deployment at facility level — the number and categories of health workers (nurses, counsellors, community health workers) posted to each facility. Critical for understanding staffing constraints that affect service delivery capacity, particularly for ART initiation and viral load monitoring.
GranularityFacility
RefreshMonthly

SyNCH

HST (ASG)
Central Chronic Medicine Dispensing and Distribution (CCMDD) programme data. Tracks active patient script counts at decentralised pickup points — a key indicator of ART adherence and programme reach. Patients who collect their ARVs through CCMDD are stable on treatment and have been “decanted” from clinic visits to community-based collection, freeing clinic capacity for new initiations and complex cases.
GranularityFacility
RefreshWeekly
Coverage418 facilities

Ritshidze

Ritshidze / TAC
Community-led monitoring (CLM) survey data collected by Treatment Action Campaign (TAC) volunteers at public health facilities. Captures the patient experience: waiting times, staff attitudes, stock-outs of ARVs and contraceptives, facility cleanliness, and whether patients were turned away. Ritshidze (“we are watching” in Tshivenda) provides a critical demand-side perspective that routine facility reporting cannot capture.
GranularityFacility
RefreshQuarterly
Coverage~400 facilities across all 9 provinces

CMS (Council for Medical Schemes)

SANAC
Medical aid patient counts from private sector. Tracks the number of patients on ART through medical scheme claims data, providing the private sector complement to public sector TIER.Net numbers. Essential for estimating total national ART coverage including both public and private sectors.

IQVIA

SANAC
Cash-paying (uninsured) patient counts from retail pharmacy dispensing data. Captures patients accessing ART through private pharmacies without medical aid coverage — a population invisible to both public sector systems and medical scheme claims.
GranularityDistrict
RefreshSemi-annually
PeriodJan-Dec 2024

Key Pops (SHIPP)

Academic collaborators
District-level size estimates for key populations: men who have sex with men (MSM), sex workers (SW), people who inject drugs (PWID), and transgender people. Essential for planning targeted HIV prevention programmes (PrEP, condom distribution) and calculating programme coverage among populations with disproportionately high HIV incidence. Produced by the South African Health Information for Policy and Practice (SHIPP) programme.
GranularityDistrict

Stats SA

Statistics South Africa
Three datasets from Statistics South Africa: Mid-year population estimates (2025) used as denominators for calculating programme coverage rates; Census 2022 providing detailed demographic profiles at district and municipal level; and Mortality & Causes of Death statistics used for validating HIV/TB mortality estimates against civil registration data.
GranularityDistrict / Province
RefreshAnnually

SABSSM

HSRC
South African National HIV Prevalence, Incidence, Behaviour and Communication Survey (version vi). The largest nationally representative household survey measuring HIV prevalence, incidence, and risk behaviours. Provides the ground-truth benchmark for estimating the number of people living with HIV, knowledge of status, and behavioural risk factors. Survey data underpins the calibration of models like Thembisa and Naomi and is used to validate routine programme data.
